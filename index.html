Welcome!

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Political Science at Vanderbilt University specializing in international politics and political methodology. My research interests include foreign aid, domestic politics and foreign policy, environment-conflict linkages, international institutions, and formal theory. My dissertation considers (1) how aid channeled through NGOs and multilateral organizations affects the survival of autocratic regimes, (2) how the design of international institutions for disaster relief affects donors' willingness to provide aid and recipient welfare, and (3) how natural disasters affect international conflict through their effects on leader survival.

I earned a M.A. in Political Science and a Graduate Certificate in Sustainability Politics & Policy from the University of New Hampshire in 2011, and graduated from Salem State University with a B.S. in Political Science in 2009.

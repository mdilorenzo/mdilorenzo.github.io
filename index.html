Welcome!

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Political Science at Vanderbilt University specializing in international politics and political methodology. My substantive research interests include domestic politics and international conflict, foreign aid, international institutions, and formal theory. My dissertation considers (1) how natural disasters' effects on leader survival influence leaders' international conflict behavior, (2) how the design of international institutions for disaster relief affects donors' willingness to provide aid and recipient welfare, and (3) how aid channeled through NGOs and multilateral organizations affects the survival of autocratic regimes. I use a combination of formal models, statistics, and simulations in my research.

I earned a M.A. in Political Science and a Graduate Certificate in Sustainability Politics & Policy from the University of New Hampshire in 2011, and graduated from Salem State University with a B.S. in Political Science in 2009.
